# US-039: Research Phase

**Epic:** AI Content Pipeline
**Priority:** P0
**Milestone:** M7 — AI Content Pipeline
**PRD Refs:** FR-201

## User Story
As a business owner, I want the AI to research my industry before generating content, so that the output reflects real industry knowledge, terminology, and best practices.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Given onboarding data is submitted, when the Research phase begins, then it analyzes: business idea, industry, audience, differentiators, and any reference URLs
- [ ] Given the Research phase, when it completes, then it produces a structured JSON research brief containing: industry terminology, common page structures, content patterns, and compliance considerations (HIPAA, ADA, legal disclaimers)
- [ ] Given the research brief, when displayed to the user, then they see "What we learned about your business" with key findings as bullet points
- [ ] Given the Research phase, when it runs, then it completes within 15 seconds (NFR-01)
- [ ] Given the research brief, when generated, then it is stored as a separate versioned record in the database (not embedded in the blueprint)
- [ ] Given research brief persistence, when the Plan phase needs it, then it can retrieve the brief without re-running Research

## Dependencies
- US-038 (AI Provider Abstraction Layer)
- US-033 (Industry-Specific Follow-up Questions)
- US-034 (Key Differentiators & Tone Selection)

## Technical Notes
- Single AI call using the provider abstraction's `generateJSON()` method
- Research brief TypeScript interface must be defined with all expected fields
- The brief becomes the foundation context for all subsequent pipeline phases
- Store in a new `research_briefs` table with foreign key to the site record
- Consider including compliance flags that later phases use for disclaimers
